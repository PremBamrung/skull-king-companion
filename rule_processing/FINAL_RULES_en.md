## 1. OVERVIEW AND COMPONENTS

**Skull King** is a trick-taking game where you bet on the exact number of tricks you think you will win each round. Try to keep your bid afloat while seizing opportunities to sink your opponents. The pirate with the highest score at the end of the game claims victory and the title of **Captain of the Seven Seas**!

## 2. SETUP AND KEY CONCEPTS

### SETUP

1. **Remove the following 8 cards** for your first few games: the blank cards (4), the Loot cards (2), the Kraken (1), and the White Whale (1). These cards are used with the advanced rules.
2. Deal each player a player aid and a set of bid reminder cards.
3. Shuffle the remaining cards.

### KEY CONCEPTS (GLOSSARY)

* **Trick:** Each player, in clockwise order, plays one card face up. The strongest card wins the trick. The won cards are gathered in front of the winner.
* **Round:** A set of tricks. The number of tricks is equal to the number of cards dealt (e.g., Round 3 = 3 cards per player = 3 tricks).
* **Suit:** The numbered cards from 1 to 14 (Green, Purple, Yellow, and Black).
* **Trump:** The black cards (**Jolly Roger**). They are stronger than all other color suits.
* **Follow Suit:** The first colored card played determines the suit to follow. Other players *must* play this color if they have it. Cards without numbers (Special cards) do not have a color.

## 3. PLAYING A ROUND

The game takes place over **10 rounds**.

### A. DEALING

* Round 1: 1 card per player.
* Round 2: 2 cards per player... and so on up to 10 cards in Round 10.
* *Note for 7-8 players:* Reduce the number of cards in rounds 9 and 10 if necessary so everyone receives the same number.

### B. BIDDING (THE "YO-HO-HO!")

After looking at your cards, estimate the exact number of tricks you think you will win.

1. Everyone knocks their fist on the table three times shouting **"Yo-ho-ho!"**.
2. On the third knock, extend a number of fingers corresponding to your bid (or a closed fist for zero).
3. Use the **bid reminder cards** to mark your bid in front of you.
4. One player notes all bids on the scorepad.

### C. PLAYING THE TRICK

The player to the left of the dealer starts the first trick.

* Play proceeds clockwise.
* You must **follow the requested suit**. If you do not have it, you can play any other color (including the black trump) or a special card.
* The winner of the trick leads the next trick.

---

## 4. CARD HIERARCHY

### COLORED CARDS

* **Classic Suits (Green, Yellow, Purple):** If multiple cards of the same color are played, the highest number wins. If a different color is played (excluding trump), it cannot win.
* **Trump (Black):** A black card beats any card of another color, regardless of its value.

### SPECIAL CARDS

These can be played at any time, even if you have the requested color.

* **Escape:** Losing to all other cards. If everyone plays an Escape, the first player wins the trick.
* **Pirate:** Beats all numbered cards. If multiple Pirates are played, the first one played wins.
* **Tigress:** The player chooses at the moment they play it if it is an **Escape** or a **Pirate**.
* **Skull King:** The King of the game. He beats all Pirates and all numbered cards.
* **Mermaid:** Beats all numbered cards. She loses against Pirates, **BUT she wins against the Skull King**.

> **[Captain's Log]:** If a Pirate, the Skull King, and a Mermaid are in the same trick, the **Mermaid always wins**, because she captures the King.

### STARTING A TRICK WITH A SPECIAL CARD

* **If it is an Escape:** The next card determines the suit to follow.
* **If it is a Character (Pirate, Mermaid, Skull King):** There is **no suit to follow**. Other players play whatever they want.

## 5. SCORING (SKULL KING SYSTEM)

You only score points if your bid is **exact**.

### BIDDING 1 OR MORE

* **Successful Bid:** +20 points per trick won (e.g., bid 3 and won 3 = 60 pts).
* **Failed Bid:** -10 points per trick difference (too many or too few). You score 0 points for your won tricks.

### BIDDING ZERO (THE "ZERO")

* **Successful Bid:** +10 points multiplied by the number of cards in the round (e.g., Round 7 successful at zero = 70 pts).
* **Failed Bid:** -10 points multiplied by the number of cards in the round (e.g., Round 9 failed = -90 pts).

### BONUS POINTS

These are only awarded if your bid is **correct**:

* **The 14:** +10 points for a colored 14, +20 points for the black 14.
* **Character Capture:**
* A Pirate captures a Mermaid: **+20 pts**.
* The Skull King captures a Pirate: **+30 pts**.
* A Mermaid captures the Skull King: **+40 pts**.

## 6. VARIANT: RASCAL SYSTEM

Ideal for a more balanced game. Each round has a potential of **10 pts per card dealt**.

* **Direct Hit (Exact Bid):** 100% of the round's points.
* **Glancing Blow (Difference of 1):** 50% of the round's points.
* **Complete Failure (Difference of 2+):** 0 points.

### Optional Rule: Buckshot vs. Cannonball

After bidding, secretly choose your mode:

* **Buckshot (Open Hand):** Standard Rascal rule (points for difference of 0 or 1).
* **Cannonball (Closed Fist):** +15 pts per card if exactly successful, 0 pts if incorrect (even by a single trick).

## 7. 2-PLAYER RULE: THE GHOST OF GREYBEARD

Shuffle and deal 3 piles. The third pile belongs to the ghost of **Greybeard**.

* Greybeard always plays in the 2nd position.
* Simply flip the top card of his deck on his turn. He does not follow suit.
* If he wins the trick, he starts the next one (flip his next card).
* His **Tigress** is always an Escape.

---

## 8. ADVANCED RULES AND EXPANSIONS

### LEVIATHANS OF THE DEEP

* **The Kraken:** Destroys the trick. All cards are discarded. No one wins. The player who would have won leads the next trick.
* **The White Whale:** Destroys special cards and turns colors into simple numbers. The highest numbered card (regardless of color) wins.

### ADDITIONAL CARDS

* **Loot:** Creates an alliance. If the giver of the loot and the winner of the trick both make their bid, they gain **+20 bonus pts**.

### PIRATE POWERS (VARIANT)

If you win a trick with a pirate, use their power immediately:

* **Rosie the Sweet:** Choose who starts the next trick.
* **Will the Bandit:** Draw 2 cards, discard 2.
* **Rascal the Gambler:** Bet 0, 10, or 20 pts (won or lost depending on your final bid).
* **Juanita Jade:** Look at the undealt cards.
* **Harry the Giant:** Modify your bid by +1 or -1 at the end of the round.

---

## 9. DURATION VARIANTS

* **Blitz Attack:** 5 rounds of 5 cards.
* **Whirlpool:** 9, 7, 5, 3, then 1 card.
* **Broadside:** 10 rounds of 10 cards.
